# NLUX — Mission, Values, and Charter

**Version:** 0.1.0-draft  
**Date:** 2026-04-03

---

## Mission Statement

NLUX makes Dutch cultural heritage collections discoverable, connectable, and open — for researchers, institutions, and the public — through shared open-source infrastructure built on international linked data standards.

---

## Vision

A Netherlands where every heritage institution, regardless of size or budget, can participate in a shared, interoperable digital discovery landscape: one where a medieval manuscript in Leiden, a scientific instrument in Haarlem, and an archaeological find in Castricum can all be found, compared, and understood in their full context.

---

## Values

### 1. Open by Default
All code is open source (Apache 2.0). All data standards are open (Linked.art, CIDOC-CRM). We do not build walls around knowledge.

### 2. Community over Commerce
NLUX is a foundation, not a product company. Decisions are made in the interest of the cultural heritage community, not shareholders or a single vendor.

### 3. Standards First
We implement international standards (Linked.art, CIDOC-CRM, IIIF, SPARQL) faithfully. We contribute improvements upstream. We do not invent proprietary formats when open ones exist.

### 4. Sustainability
We design for the long term: low dependencies, documented everything, upstream compatibility. A project that outlives its founder.

### 5. Accessibility and Inclusion
Our platform should be usable by institutions with limited IT resources, developers without linked data backgrounds, and end users speaking Dutch, English, or other languages.

### 6. Transparency
Finances, roadmaps, decisions, and discussions are public. We acknowledge our limitations and uncertainties.

## Charter

### Name
The project is called **NLUX** (working name; formally: *NLUX – Nederlandse Linked Erfgoed Uitwisseling* or similar, to be confirmed at incorporation).

### Legal Form
A Dutch *Stichting* (foundation), incorporated under Dutch law, with a registered office in the Netherlands.

### Purpose (Statutair Doel)
The foundation's statutory purpose is:
> To develop, maintain, and promote open-source software and open data standards that enable Dutch cultural heritage institutions to share and discover their collections online, and to do everything related to or conducive to this purpose.

### Membership and Governance
- The Stichting is governed by a **Board** (bestuur) of 3–7 members.
- The Board includes a chair, treasurer, and secretary.
- An informal **User Group** (vertegenwoordiging van deelnemende instellingen) advises the Board.
- Major decisions (licence changes, mergers, dissolution) require a supermajority (2/3) of the Board.

### Intellectual Property Policy
- Code produced by NLUX is published under Apache License 2.0.
- The NLUX name and logo are trademarks of the Stichting.
- Data contributed by institutions is not transferred to NLUX; institutions grant a licence for aggregation and display.

### Code of Conduct
NLUX adopts the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/) Code of Conduct for all community spaces (GitHub, mailing lists, events).

### Dissolution
In the event of dissolution, any remaining assets are transferred to an organisation with a compatible mission (e.g. NDE, a comparable open cultural heritage foundation).
